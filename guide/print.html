Home

The course as a journey

Home lays the course out week by week. Each station shows the week's title and its guiding question, the one question worth carrying through everything you read that week.

  1. 1Click any week station to open that week's page.
  2. 2Coming back later? The site remembers where you were on this device and offers to pick up where you left off.
The week page, block by block

Anatomy of a week

Every week page runs in the order the week is meant to run. Work it top to bottom.

1
Time budget, under the title, roughly how the week's work divides up.
2
Overview & How this week works, read this first; it tells you what to do and in what order.
3
Purpose & Outcomes, what you should be able to do by the end. Test yourself against these sentences.
4
Guiding Questions, carry at least one with you into the readings.
5
Key Concepts, each explained and cited to that week's reading, so you always know where an idea comes from.
6
Readings, full citation and a button. Open access, Seneca Library, Read on Blackboard, or Revisit from an earlier week.
7
The Walkthrough, a slide deck embedded on the page. Arrow keys move through it, or open it fullscreen.
8
The Activity, press Start and follow the prompts. Practice, not assessment.
9
Reflect, a short prompt (one or two sentences) to close the week.
10
You can now, the week's closing checklist. Tick what you can genuinely do; anything left unticked shows exactly what to revisit. Reset any time.
Library of Readings

Every reading, as a card

The library holds every course reading: what it is, what it argues, how long it takes, and how to reach it.

  1. 1Search any author, title, or idea.
  2. 2Filter to one week with the week strip, or browse by format.
  3. 3Click a card for the reading's page: core idea, plain-language abstract, level and length, and where the text lives.
  4. 4Press the red button to open the reading. A Source page button appears where a separate source or DOI exists.
  5. 5Bookmark a reading to your Saved shelf. It stays on this device so you can return before an assessment.
Compare Reading Concepts

Hold up to three readings side by side

  1. 1Open Compare from the menu, or press Compare on any reading page.
  2. 2Pick up to three readings. Each becomes a column: week, year, origin, length, level, and core idea.
  3. 3With two or more selected, press Show a worked weaving. The site models ONE way of holding the readings together, fully attributed, with both ways of knowing kept whole. It always ends by handing the weaving back to you, because your weaving is the one that counts.
  4. 4With three, it takes the pairings one at a time, then asks what only appears when all three are on the table.
  5. 5Remove a reading with the × on its column, or Clear all to start over.

Self-check

Practice, never scored

  • Pick a week, or take all weeks together.
  • Flip the cards: term on the front, definition and citation on the back.
  • Run the short quizzes; each question explains its answer. Nothing is recorded, it exists so you know what to reread before it matters.
Weekly Walkthrough hub. Every week's deck also lives in one place. Open it from the menu and pick the week to rewatch without hunting.
Etuaptmumk

Two-Eyed Seeing on the site

This course is framed by Etuaptmumk, Two-Eyed Seeing, Mi'kmaw Elder Albert Marshall's principle of seeing with the strengths of Indigenous ways of knowing in one eye and Western ways of knowing in the other, both kept whole. Every reading on the site is labelled with its perspective. Neither is ranked above the other, and the site never blends them into one voice.

The two attributed eyes studio

See with both eyes

  3. 3Press Save my work to the Personal Cartography to download your responses as a Word document you can keep building on all term.
Personal Cartography

The map

The map anchors the course's Indigenous scholars to their self-identified nations, each anchor linked to its reading. It is a teaching aid, not a legal or definitive boundary; for authoritative territories and treaties, consult the Nations themselves and sources such as Native-Land.ca. Practical notes

Good to know

Works on phones, tablets, and laptops, and is fully keyboard-navigable.
Your saved shelf, checklists, and progress live only in your own browser, on your own device.
Clearing browser data clears them, and they do not follow you between devices.
If the site looks stale after an announced update, refresh the page once.
The one rule that matters
When in doubt, Blackboard wins.

The companion site is your reading room. Blackboard is the course of record: announcements, discussions, every submission, and every grade live there, and the course outline is the authoritative schedule.
